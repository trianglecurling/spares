# Sabbaticals

## Purpose

A sabbatical allows an eligible returning member to temporarily step away from a
standard league while preserving their right to return later.

Sabbaticals are intended for members who cannot play in a league for a session
but do not want to permanently give up their spot.

---

## Eligibility

A curler may request sabbatical only if they are eligible for a guaranteed
return spot in that league.

Sabbatical eligibility requires:

- The current registration period is in priority state.
- The curler is eligible for guaranteed return to the league.
- The league supports sabbaticals.
- The league is not a bring-your-own-team league.
- The spot is not a temporary sabbatical-fill spot.
- The curler does not exceed the protected-claim limit.
- The curler does not exceed the simultaneous sabbatical limit.
- The sabbatical duration limit has not been exceeded, unless staff overrides
  the limit.

A curler cannot request sabbatical for a league they are merely joining from a
waitlist.

A curler cannot immediately request sabbatical for a newly offered waitlist
spot.

A curler cannot request sabbatical outside priority registration.

---

## Protected-claim limit

A curler may have at most two protected league claims in a session.

Protected claims are:

- Guaranteed return
- Sabbatical

Examples:

- Return to two leagues: allowed.
- Return to one league and take sabbatical for one league: allowed.
- Take sabbatical for two leagues: allowed.
- Return to two leagues and take sabbatical for a third league: not allowed.
- Take sabbatical for three leagues: not allowed.

---

## Simultaneous sabbatical limit

A curler may be on sabbatical for at most two leagues at the same time.

This aligns with the protected-claim limit.

---

## Membership requirement

A sabbatical-only registrant does not need to purchase regular membership.

If a registrant is only taking sabbatical and has no non-guaranteed items,
payment for the sabbatical fee may be collected immediately.

If a registrant takes sabbatical and also has non-guaranteed league selections,
payment may be deferred and combined into one later invoice.

---

## Sabbatical fee

A sabbatical fee is charged for each league held on sabbatical.

The sabbatical fee is paid every session that the curler remains on sabbatical.

The sabbatical fee is not discount-eligible.

Student, reciprocal, winter-only, or other discounts do not reduce the
sabbatical fee.

---

## Sabbatical-fill discount

When another curler temporarily fills a sabbatical spot, that curler receives a
discount equal to the sabbatical fee.

The sabbatical-fill discount is always the full sabbatical fee.

It is not reduced by percentage discount sequencing rules.

---

## Duration limit

The default sabbatical duration limit is three years.

The value must be configurable.

The limit is the same for all leagues unless tenant configuration later
supports otherwise.

The sabbatical clock starts on the first day of league play for the first league
session in which the sabbatical begins.

A curler is ineligible to extend sabbatical into any league whose final game
date is on or after the configured duration limit.

Example:

- Sabbatical starts with a league whose first day of play is October 1, 2026.
- The sabbatical limit is three years.
- The curler may not claim sabbatical for a league whose final game date is on
  or after October 1, 2029.

Staff may override the duration limit for extenuating circumstances.

Overrides should be recorded and auditable.

---

## Expiration

When a sabbatical expires, the curler must either return to the league or
release the spot.

If the curler does not return, the spot is released permanently.

After release, the curler must join the waitlist to get back into the league.

The system should make the consequences clear before a curler makes a decision
that releases a sabbatical-protected spot.

---

## Temporary sabbatical-fill spots

When a curler takes sabbatical, the vacant playing spot may be offered to
another curler.

Rules:

- The fill-in curler is not guaranteed permanent return to that spot.
- The fill-in curler receives a discount equal to the sabbatical fee.
- The fill-in curler keeps their position on the waitlist.
- The original sabbatical curler cannot return mid-session.
- The temporary spot is re-evaluated each session.
- If the original sabbatical curler extends sabbatical, the temporary spot may
  again be offered from the waitlist.
- If a permanent spot opens, the temporary fill-in curler may be offered that
  permanent spot according to normal waitlist order.

The temporary nature of the spot must be made clear to the user before they
accept.

---

## Relationship to waitlists

Temporary sabbatical-fill spots are offered through the waitlist process.

Permanent vacancies are filled before temporary sabbatical-fill vacancies.

Declining a temporary sabbatical-fill spot counts as a normal waitlist decline.

Rules:

- First decline keeps position.
- Second decline moves the waitlist entry to the bottom.
- Decline count is per waitlist instance.

---

## BYOT leagues

Bring-your-own-team leagues do not participate in the sabbatical system.

A curler cannot request sabbatical for a BYOT league.

---

## Staff overrides

Staff may override the sabbatical duration limit for extenuating circumstances.

Staff may also manually resolve unusual sabbatical issues.

Staff overrides should be audited, especially when they affect:

- Duration limit
- Spot release
- Return eligibility
- Temporary fill-in status
- Payment status
